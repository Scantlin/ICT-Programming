About Mechanical Waves

Mechanical waves require a medium (solid, liquid, or gas) to propagate. There are two main types:

  • Transverse waves: Particles move perpendicular to wave direction (e.g., waves on a string)
  • Longitudinal waves: Particles move parallel to wave direction (e.g., sound waves)

Key wave properties include:

  • Amplitude (A) - Maximum displacement from equilibrium
  • Wavelength (Î») - Distance between consecutive peaks
  • Frequency (f) - Number of oscillations per second
  • Period (T) - Time for one complete oscillation (T = 1/f)
  • Wave speed (v) - Speed of wave propagation (v = Î»f)
